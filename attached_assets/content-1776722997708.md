## What is a product detail page?

A PDP, or product detail page, in [digital commerce](https://www.shopify.com/blog/digital-commerce) is a landing page that provides comprehensive information about a specific product. It should include [product descriptions](https://www.shopify.com/blog/8211159-9-simple-ways-to-write-product-descriptions-that-sell), images, pricing, and customer reviews. Optimized PDPs include high-quality [product photography](https://www.shopify.com/blog/ecommerce-photography), detailed product descriptions, and user reviews to enhance the shopping experience and boost conversion rates.

Although [ecommerce](https://www.shopify.com/blog/what-is-ecommerce) product detail pages can’t replace an in-store experience where customers physically interact with your product, they offset the [advantages and disadvantages of ecommerce](https://www.shopify.com/blog/advantages-and-disadvantages-of-ecommerce). Effective PDPs help customers understand the key features and benefits of the product, providing a rich online shopping experience.

PDPs can also support [search engine optimization (SEO)](https://www.shopify.com/blog/ecommerce-seo-beginners-guide) efforts, improving your visibility on [search engine results pages (SERPs)](https://www.shopify.com/blog/serp) that bring you new customers.

## PLP and PDP in ecommerce: differences

A product listing page (PLP) shows all the products available in a given [product category](https://www.shopify.com/blog/product-category). Shoppers can scan, filter, and compare products at a glance. A PDP is more like a deep dive for one SKU. It provides rich images, video, specs, pricing, and [calls to action](https://www.shopify.com/blog/what-is-a-call-to-action) to encourage the visitor to buy that particular product.

Here’s a quick breakdown of the main differences between PLPs and PDPs.

|              | PLP                                                                       | PDP                                                                                                 |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Goal         | Help visitors locate product sets quickly.                                | Persuade visitors to buy a single product.                                                          |
| Content      | Thumbnail image, price, product title, ratings, filters, infinite scroll. | Video/360-view, product description, availability, shipping and returns info, reviews, cross-sells. |
| Intent stage | Exploration or consideration.                                             | Consideration and decision.                                                                         |
| SEO focus    | Broad keywords like “men’s running shoes.”                                | Long tail keywords like “Nike Pegasus 41 size 10.”                                                  |
| Key metrics  | Click-through rate to PDP, bounce rate, dwell time.                       | Conversion rate, cart adds, average order value.                                                    |

In practice, [direct-to-consumer](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/direct-to-consumer) brand Gymshark’s PLP surfaces different collections through smart [ecommerce merchandising](https://www.shopify.com/blog/ecommerce-merchandising). This lets shoppers filter products and sort by price, features, fit, activity, discount, and more.

[Shadow seamless t-shirt PDP](https://www.gymshark.com/products/gymshark-shadow-seamless-t-shirt-black-aw24) dives into the fabric, sizing, colors, and delivery information to inspire shoppers to buy that product. They can easily add the product to their cart, read reviews, and pay in installments.

Here are the essential elements that go into creating a compelling product detail page for your ecommerce website.

### Product title

The product title helps customers quickly identify if the product is what they’re looking for and can influence their decision to explore more details. The title typically is the largest and most prominent text on the page. Titles should be concise, clear, and descriptive to reinforce your [differentiation strategy](https://www.shopify.com/blog/differentiation-strategy).

A title that’s optimized for search engines incorporates [keywords](https://www.shopify.com/blog/how-to-do-keyword-research) that potential customers might use when searching for the product.

### Product description

Product descriptions provide a broad overview of the product, typically sitting beneath the product title. These descriptions should contain essential information, including key features and materials, as well as your [unique value proposition](https://www.shopify.com/blog/unique-value-proposition).

The language should be appealing and aligned with your brand’s voice. [Effective product descriptions](https://www.shopify.com/blog/8211159-9-simple-ways-to-write-product-descriptions-that-sell) accomplish two primary goals: help customers make an informed decision and persuade them to buy.

### Additional product details or specifications

These sections provide further product attributes or technical specifications, such as sizes and color options, dimensions, weight, volume, brand, or model. Details like these help customers make purchase decisions, which can reduce returns, [churn rate](https://www.shopify.com/blog/churn-rate-in-ecommerce), and customer complaints.

### High-quality digital assets

No product detail page would be complete without high-quality photos or video. High-resolution [product images](https://www.shopify.com/blog/12206313-the-ultimate-diy-guide-to-beautiful-product-photography) give a comprehensive view of the product from different angles. It’s also helpful to set up your PDP to toggle between images of product variants when site visitors change colors, materials, or sizes on the page. To capture product details, brands rely on a mix of artificial and [natural lighting techniques](https://www.shopify.com/blog/natural-lighting-product-photography) in product photography.

[Product videos](https://www.shopify.com/blog/product-video) are another way to bring your product to life and earn the trust of prospective customers. They offer an engaging way to learn more about how a product works, along with its features, benefits, and uses.

“We have learned so much from our customers,” says Morgan Cros, founder of [Original Duckhead](https://originalduckhead.com/). “One of the big changes that we’ve made, besides introducing new products, was, for example, customers were telling us we need to see the product in use, and all of our imagery online was still life. So a couple of years ago we did our first photo shoot with models. And so that was, you know, something that seems so obvious that we just hadn’t addressed because things were working without it.”

![Product page for magenta compact umbrella with automatic open-close function.](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0070/7032/files/originalduckhead_08ed3c01-b6a1-470c-9f66-395e246c7b11.png?v=1747986478)Original Duckhead’s PDP uses high-quality product and lifestyle imagery to showcase a magenta umbrella.

### Price

The product price is often the most important consideration for buyers. Place the price near the [call-to-action](https://www.shopify.com/blog/17156160-7-inspiring-ecommerce-call-to-action-examples-and-why-they-work) (CTA) button to place an item in a [shopping cart](https://www.shopify.com/blog/shopping-cart). You can display discounts or price reductions, as well as different promotions, subscriptions, or [buy now, pay later](https://www.shopify.com/blog/buy-now-pay-later) (BNPL) payment options. It’s also important to mention if shipping costs or taxes affect the final price.

### Availability

Include product availability on a product detail page to let customers know if the product is in stock. If stock is limited for a particular product, displaying this can encourage a purchase.

### Quantity to purchase

Include a small drop-down list or other order-quantity input field for selecting the number of items for purchase. You can set minimum and maximum order quantities here as well.

### Call to action

Calls to action (CTAs) are short, concise statements designed to get customers to act and are therefore one of the PDP’s most essential elements for conversion.

Most CTA buttons on product detail pages encourage customers to purchase or add to their carts—Add to Cart, Buy Now, or Customers Also Bought, for example—and should be highly visible with simple copy.

### Return and exchange policy details

Include relevant and transparent information about return or exchange conditions, or if a product is final sale. [Return and exchange policies](https://www.shopify.com/blog/return-policy) can make customers feel more comfortable purchasing from you, enhancing trust and building loyalty.

### Fulfillment options

Include shipping information and timelines or other ways customers can receive your products, such as [buy online, pickup in-store](https://www.shopify.com/retail/bopis) (BOPIS). Since unexpected shipping costs are one of the leading reasons for [cart abandonment](https://www.shopify.com/blog/shopping-cart-abandonment), include exact shipping options and fees on the PDP, if possible.

## Common PDP mistakes to avoid

Avoiding missteps protects your hard-earned traffic and profit margins, especially when a small conversion lift or rise in [average order value](https://www.shopify.com/blog/average-order-value) can lead to more revenue.

Here are the mistakes to avoid when creating your PDP.

### Insufficient product information

Shoppers bail when specifications, sizing, or social proof are missing. [Baymard’s 2025 benchmark](https://baymard.com/blog/current-state-ecommerce-product-page-ux) found that 67% of sites lacked buyer-generated visuals, which can leave customers feeling uncertain about completing their purchase. Add a spec table, detailed sizing, and UGC photos above the fold to reassure shoppers that they are getting the right product. [CPG ecommerce](https://www.shopify.com/blog/cpg-ecommerce) brands, for example, include ingredients and nutrition panels.

### Poor quality images and media

Low-resolution media on a PDP destroys the trust built during your shoppers’ journey. When [Peepers Eyewear upgraded](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/fashion-conversion-rate-optimization) its PDP media with larger, more vibrant photos, it lifted conversions by 30%. Invest in high-quality [product photography](https://www.shopify.com/blog/12206313-the-ultimate-diy-guide-to-beautiful-product-photography), 360-degree experiences, and contextual videos to show texture and scale.

### Confusing navigation and user flow

PDPs cluttered with competing CTAs or buried information create buyer friction. In fact, [18% of customers](https://baymard.com/lists/cart-abandonment-rate) will abandon their cart if the checkout process is too complicated. Shopify Themes have these [ecommerce navigation](https://www.shopify.com/enterprise/blog/ecommerce-navigation) best practices with [predictive search](https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/online-store/storefront-search/predictive-search), multilevel menus, search filtering, and country/language selection.

### Neglecting mobile optimization

The [latest ecommerce conversion rate benchmarks](https://www.smartinsights.com/ecommerce/ecommerce-analytics/ecommerce-conversion-rates/) show that while mobile device usage is at an all-time high (75%), mobile conversion rates are lower than on desktop. There’s clearly still work to be done for mobile optimization.

You can customize mobile-specific settings like the number of columns in product screens and gain access to accessibility features like customized text and keyboard support for mobility impairments.

## What is PDP in ecommerce FAQ

### What is a PLP in ecommerce?

Product listing pages (PLPs) are pages on an ecommerce site that display a group of products based on a category or a visitor’s search query. Users can access individual ecommerce product detail pages from a product listing page.

### What is a PDP on Shopify?

Within Shopify and other ecommerce platforms, PDP stands for product detail page. These web pages provide customers with important product details, including product benefits, features, pricing, photos, videos, ratings, reviews, and other relevant information customers want to help them make a purchase decision.

### What makes a good product detail page?

A good product detail page provides customers with all the relevant information they need, while offering them an engaging online shopping experience. Effective product detail pages include essential elements such as price, product photos or videos, availability, and a call to action. Best practices for designing PDPs include providing social proof, employing cross-selling tactics, and ensuring they’re optimized for mobile.

### Do product detail pages impact SEO?

Yes. As the most common pages on many ecommerce sites, product detail pages can positively affect your SEO efforts by including relevant keywords in the page’s content, title tag, meta descriptions, and alt tags for photos and videos, among other tactics.

### How can AI improve product detail pages?

AI tools can make a number of improvements on product detail pages. For example, you can use Shopify Magic to write product descriptions that are on brand and create alt text for your images. Apps like Nosto can provide personalized product recommendations and surface predictive sizing.

### How do personalized product detail pages affect conversion rates?

Personalized product pages tweak certain text, media, and offers based on each shopper’s behavior or customer profile. A study from [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/mckinsey-explainers/what-is-personalization) found that personalization can reduce customer acquisition costs by as much as 50% and lift revenue anywhere between 5% and 15%.

### What PDP trends should ecommerce businesses watch in 2025?

Key PDP trends to watch in 2025 include:

- **3D/AR product previews:** AI-powered product pages can let customers try on products before buying them. Shoppers can spin, zoom, and put products in their environments virtually.
- **Carbon-impact badges:** Products making sustainability claims average 28% higher growth. Tools like [Shopify Planet](https://apps.shopify.com/stories/spotlight-app-planet) enable carbon-neutral shipping and badges for PDPs.
- **First-party data and personalization:** Quizzes, wish lists, and loyalty profiles personalize content without relying on third-party cookies.
